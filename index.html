About Us

Who are we and what is our purpose?

Project Osiris focuses on providing comprehensive solutions to farmers, improving agricultural practices and optimizing the use of natural resources. Through training, access to sustainable technologies, and personalized advice, Osiris aims to increase productivity, reduce costs, and minimize environmental impact. Additionally, it promotes the use of renewable energy and encourages innovative farming techniques to address the challenges of climate change, ensuring a more resilient and sustainable agricultural production. In regions like Mexico, where agriculture plays a crucial role in the economy, such initiatives can help boost both local and national food security.

Resources

Agriculture and Water Management Pathfinder

Drought, vegetation health, and soil moisture all can be tracked remotely. This data pathfinder provides links to NASA Earth observations that help address issues including water management for irrigation, crop-type identification and land use, and drought preparedness.

Floods Data Pathfinder

Floods are one of the most costly natural disasters. NASA provides many datasets and tools that can aid with decisions regarding flood response and mitigation.

Extreme Heat Data Pathfinder

Abnormally hot and/or humid weather lasting a few days to weeks at a time is occurring more frequently in locations around the world. These events can have detrimental impacts on farms. NASA data can aid with forecasting and monitoring extreme heat events.

Nasa Acres

Established in March 2023, NASA Acres Consortium is NASA’s U.S.-focused agriculture and food security program. NASA Acres focuses on applying satellite EO information to the most pressing agricultural and food security challenges facing U.S. farmers, ranchers, and agrifood system stakeholders. NASA Acres works with people across the agriculture sector to develop EO-based data and tools that strengthen U.S. agriculture.

Nasa Harvest

NASA Harvest is a consortium of multidisciplinary and multisectoral actors from around the world, led and implemented by the Harvest Hub at the University of Maryland. This consortium brings together world-renown experts working on environmental, economic, and social aspects of agriculture in universities, research centers, industry, space agencies, humanitarian organizations, ministries of agriculture, and more.

Tools

Application for Extracting and Exploring Analysis Ready Samples (AppEEARS)

The Application for Extracting and Exploring Analysis Ready Samples (AppEEARS) offers a simple and efficient way to access and transform geospatial data from a variety of federal data archives. AppEEARS enables users to extract specific data from geospatial datasets using spatial, temporal, and band/layer parameters. Visit the AppEEARS Getting Started to learn more about workflows, viewing results, interacting with results, and downloading requests.

Worldview

The NASA Worldview data visualization application provides the capability to interactively browse more than 1,000 global, full-resolution satellite imagery layers and download the underlying data.

Giovanni

NASA’s Giovanni is an online application that enables the display and analysis of geophysical parameters. There are many options for analysis. For more information on choosing a type of plot, see the Giovanni User Manual.

NASA Earthdata Search

Earthdata Search is NASA's tool for searching for and discovering NASA Earth science data as well as relevant data available through U.S. and international agencies.

Global Agriculture Monitoring System (GLAM)

GLAM is a web-based platform that enables near-real-time monitoring of global croplands, primarily using NASA MODIS satellite data. This tool is being redesigned to incorporate new datasets and improve its speed, flexibility, and computing power.

VEDA (Visualization, Exploration, and Data Analysis)

VEDA is NASA's open-source Earth Science data analysis platform in the cloud.

U.S. Drought Monitor

The U.S. Drought Monitor is a weekly map of drought conditions used by policymakers to help determine drought relief allocations and declarations of drought. NASA satellites provide data about water availability to the U.S. Drought Monitor.

Crop Condition and Soil Moisture Analytics (Crop-CASMA)

Crop CASMA provides access to high-resolution data from NASA’s Soil Moisture Active Passive (SMAP) mission and the Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer (MODIS) instrument in an easy-to-use format. Soil moisture data are critical for professionals in the agriculture and natural resources sectors who use soil moisture in tandem with other data to plan crop planting, forecast yields, track droughts or floods, and improve weather forecasts. This application offers a suite of services such as a Web Map Service (WMS), Web Coverage Service (WCS), and Web Processing Services (WPS).

Datasets

Earthdata Login

Provides free and immediate access to thousands of NASA Earth data products covering all Earth science disciplines and topic areas for researchers, applied science users, application developers, and the general public. Each participant will need a login to download data. Registration is easy and free of charge, as is data access.

Earthdata Forum

If you have specific questions about how to use data, tools, or resources, please visit the Earthdata Forum. Here, you can interact with other data users and NASA subject matter experts on a variety of Earth science research and applications topics.

NASA's Earth Science Data Systems Backgrounders

Covers topics like sensor characteristics, resolutions, and data processing techniques.

Applied Remote Sensing Training (ARSET) Program

Offers in-depth training for agricultural applications (and other applications) for further exploration in remote sensing.
